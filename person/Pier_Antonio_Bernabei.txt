#Introduction#
Pier Antonio Bernabei (flourished mid-16th century) was an Italian painter also known as Della Casa.

A native of Parma, Pier Antonio Bernabei was a follower of the style of Correggio. Among his best-known works is a frescoed view of Paradise on the cupola of Santa Maria del Quartiere.[1] Another renowned fresco is the Assumption for a chapel at the church of San Martino at Traversetolo.[2] There are other works by this master at the Carmelites, and at other public places in Parma.
