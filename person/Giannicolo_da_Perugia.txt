#Name#
Giannicolo da Perugia

#Life Time#
1478-1544

#Introduction#
Giannicolo da Perugia, who is also known as Giovanni Nicolò Manni or Giannicola di Paolo (c. 1478 – 1544) was an Italian painter of the Renaissance period, active mainly in Perugia. He was born in Città della Pieve. His most prominent work is the Madonna delle Grazie in the Duomo of Perugia. He was a pupil of Pietro Perugino, but was also influenced by Raphael.[1]
